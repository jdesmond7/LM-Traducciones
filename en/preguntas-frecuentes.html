Common questions answered so you can start your process with confidence.

What is a sworn translator, and when is one required?

A professional certified by the judiciary to produce translations with official validity. Such translations are required for legal, immigration, academic, and commercial procedures before Mexican or foreign authorities.

How is a translation validated before authorities?

Translations bear the sworn translator’s signature, seal, and registration number, giving them full validity before courts, consulates, universities, and government institutions.

How long does a certified translation take?

Timing depends on length and complexity. Typically 1 to 5 business days. Rush options are available within 24 to 72 hours when possible.

Which documents can be translated with official validity?

Contracts, birth certificates, diplomas, degrees, academic records, notarial powers, judgments, patents, financial statements, and more—always with sworn translator certification.
