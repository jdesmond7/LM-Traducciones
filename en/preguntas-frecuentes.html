What is a Court-certified translator and when is one required?

A Court-certified translator is a professional authorized by the judicial authorities to issue translations with official legal validity. Certified translations are required for legal, immigration, academic, and commercial procedures before Mexican or foreign authorities.

How is a certified translation validated by authorities?

Certified translations include the translator’s signature, official seal, and registration number, which grant them full legal validity before courts, consulates, universities, and government institutions.

How long does a certified translation take?

The timeframe depends on the length and complexity of the document. In general, delivery takes between 1 and 5 business days. For urgent cases, express service is available within 24 to 72 hours.

What documents can be translated with official validity?

Contracts, birth certificates, diplomas, academic transcripts, powers of attorney, patents, financial statements, and many other documents can be translated with official certification.
